Spicy Salmon Bowl over Brown Rice

Description

Salmon, brown rice, fresh crunchy vegetables, with a spicy sauce that will perk up your taste buds...that's a good, good, gooodrecipe.


Ingredients

For the Salmon

  • 4 (4 ounce) salmon fillets
  • 1/4 cup soy sauce
  • 2 tablespoons avovado oil
  • 2 tablespoons chili-garlic sauce, or more to taste
  • 2 teaspoons grated fresh ginger
  • 1 teaspoon honey, or to taste
  • 1/2 teaspoon sesamen oil
  • 1 medium lime, juiced

For the Bowls

  • 2 cups cooked brown rice
  • 1 cup matchstick carrots
  • 1 cup snow peas, trimmed
  • 1 medium avocado, sliced
  • 1/2 medium red bell pepper, julienned
  • 3 stalks green onion, chopped
  • 1/4 cup natural creamy peanut butter
  • 1 tablespoon rice wine vinegar
  • 1/2 teaspoon garlic powder
  • 1 tablespoon water, or as needed
  • 2 teaspoons sesame seeds

Steps

  1. Preheat the oven to 350 degrees F (175 degrees C). Line a sheet pan with aluminum foil and place salmon fillets on top.
  2. Stir soy sauce, avocado oil, chili-garlic sauce, ginger, honey, sesame oil, and lime juice together in a small bowl; brush over salmon fillets. Set remaining sauce aside for dressing.
  3. Bake salmon in the center of the preheated oven until it flakes easily with a fork, about 15 minutes. Remove from the oven and let cool, about 15 minutes.
  4. Divide rice evenly between 4 bowls and top with salmon fillets. Distribute carrots, snow peas, avocado, and bell peppers evenly between the bowls; sprinkle green onoin over top.
  5. Stir reserved soy sauce mixture with peanut butter, rice wine viegar, and garlic powder; add water until dressing reaches desired consistency. Drizzle over the bowls and sprinkle with sesame seeds.
